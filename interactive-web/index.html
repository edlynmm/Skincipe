DID YOU KNOW

There are some good ingredients for your skin concerns that may worsen your skin condition if you apply it all at once with several other ingredients

When using Alpha Hydroxy Acids (AHAs) for better skin texture, avoid combining them with certain ingredients to prevent irritation and maintain effectiveness:
  1. 1. BHAs (e.g., salicylic acid): Use AHAs and BHAs separately to reduce the risk of irritation.
  2. 2. Retinoids (e.g., retinol, Retin-A): Alternate AHAs and retinoids on different days to minimize skin sensitivity.
  3. 3. Vitamin C (L-ascorbic acid): Apply AHAs in the evening and vitamin C in the morning to prevent skin irritation.
  4. 4. Niacinamide (vitamin B3): Use them separately or choose products that blend niacinamide and AHAs in lower concentrations.
  5. 5. Benzoyl Peroxide: If needed, use AHAs and benzoyl peroxide at different times or as directed by a dermatologist.
  6. 6. Physical Exfoliants: Avoid using abrasive scrubs on the same day as AHAs to protect your skin's barrier.
  7. 7. Fragranced Products: Opt for fragrance-free products to reduce the risk of skin sensitivity.
Ok! When using Beta Hydroxy Acid (BHA), or salicylic acid, in your skincare routine, it's essential to avoid combining it with certain ingredients to prevent potential irritation or reduced effectiveness. Here's a list of ingredients not recommended to be used with BHA:
  1. 1. Alpha Hydroxy Acids (AHAs): Combining BHA with AHAs like glycolic or lactic acid can increase the risk of skin irritation. Use them separately at different times.
  2. 2. Vitamin C (L-Ascorbic Acid): High concentrations of vitamin C, especially L-ascorbic acid, can be irritating when used with BHA. Use them at different times or on alternate days.
  3. 3. Retinoids (Retinol, Retin-A): BHA and retinoids may increase skin sensitivity and irritation when used together. Consider using them on different days or in separate routines.
  4. 4. Physical Exfoliants: Avoid using physical exfoliants (scrubs, brushes, abrasive cleansers) simultaneously with BHA to prevent excessive exfoliation and irritation.
  5. 5. Sulfur: Sulfur-based products may interact negatively with BHA, potentially causing irritation. It's best to use them separately.
  6. 6. Benzoyl Peroxide: Combining BHA with benzoyl peroxide, a common acne treatment, can lead to dryness and irritation. Use them in different routines.
  7. 7. Niacinamide: While most people can use niacinamide and BHA together without issues, some may experience flushing or temporary redness. Patch-test and introduce them gradually if needed.
Ok! Vitamin C is a potent skincare ingredient, but it can be sensitive to certain combinations. Avoid combining it with:
  1. 1. Niacinamide: They may cancel each other out; use at different times.
  2. 2. Retinol: Applying them separately reduces potential irritation.
  3. 3. Alpha/Beta Hydroxy Acids: Apply vitamin C first to maintain effectiveness.
  4. 4. Benzoyl Peroxide: Using them together can cause skin irritation. Be cautious and test products gradually.
Ok! Niacinamide is a versatile skincare ingredient, but it should be used with caution alongside certain others to avoid potential issues:
  1. 1. Vitamin C: High concentrations of L-ascorbic acid may cause skin flushing or irritation when combined with niacinamide. Consider using them at different times.
  2. 2. Alpha Hydroxy Acids (AHAs): AHAs like glycolic or lactic acid can lower niacinamide's pH, potentially reducing its effectiveness. Use them in separate routines or on alternate days.
  3. 3. Beta Hydroxy Acids (BHAs): BHAs, such as salicylic acid, may also affect niacinamide's pH. Apply them at different times or on different days.
  4. 4. Retinoids: Combining niacinamide with certain retinoids, like tretinoin, may cause irritation. Use them separately, with one in your morning routine and the other at night.
  5. 5. Benzoyl Peroxide: While there's no concrete evidence of conflict, some may experience temporary flushing or irritation using niacinamide and benzoyl peroxide together. If you do use them together, apply them at different times.
Ok! Certain ingredients can be irritating when combined with retinoids. Here's a list to be cautious of:
  1. 1. AHAs and BHAs: Like glycolic and salicylic acids, they can increase skin sensitivity.
  2. 2. Benzoyl Peroxide: May be too harsh and cause dryness.
  3. 3. Vitamin C: Best used separately due to potential irritation.
  4. 4. Essential Oils: Some, like citrus oils, can make skin more sensitive.
  5. 5. Fragrance: Opt for fragrance-free products.
  6. 6. Strong Physical Exfoliants: They can be abrasive; gentle exfoliation is better.
  7. 7. Prescription Medications: Consult your healthcare provider for interactions.
  8. 8. Multiple Active Ingredients: Avoid overloading your routine.
Ok! Benzoyl peroxide is a common acne treatment, but it can be irritating when combined with certain other ingredients. Avoid combining benzoyl peroxide with:
  1. 1. Retinoids: Using benzoyl peroxide and retinoids (like tretinoin) together can lead to excessive dryness, redness, and irritation.
  2. 2. Alpha Hydroxy Acids (AHAs) and Beta Hydroxy Acids (BHAs): Mixing benzoyl peroxide with AHAs or BHAs can increase the likelihood of skin irritation.
  3. 3. Vitamin C: Benzoyl peroxide can oxidize vitamin C, rendering it less effective.
  4. 4. Sulfur: Combining benzoyl peroxide and sulfur may increase the risk of skin irritation.
